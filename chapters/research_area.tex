\section*{About}

 {Dr. Ariel Noyman is an architect, urban designer, and urban scientist whose work explores how design and technology can ~\textbf{democratize data-driven urban processes}. With two decades of experience spanning professional practice, urban science research, and teaching, he develops tools and methods that bridge urban analytics, simulation, and participatory design. At MIT, Noyman developed CityScope, an Urban Human-Computer Interaction platform that integrates real-time simulation and urban analytics to support evidence-based, collaborative planning. CityScope has been deployed in more than twenty cities worldwide, addressing challenges such as refugee housing allocation in Hamburg, resident-driven transit planning in Boston, and urban recovery strategies in war-damaged Kharkiv. To extend this work globally, Noyman helped establish a global network of City Science Labs that serve as living laboratories for urban innovation. Noyman`s design and research have received international recognition, including a European Union UrbanAct Award for FindingPlaces, first place in the Rebuild by Design competition for post-hurricane recovery, and first prize in the Museum of Tolerance design competition in Jerusalem. He has taught design studios and urban technology seminars at MIT, Cornell, and Northeastern University. Noyman holds a PhD and SM from MIT and a B.Arch (cum laude) from Bezalel Academy.}

\subsection*{Research Areas}
\textit{Democratizing Urban Processes:} Data-driven approaches to understanding, designing, and collaborative city planning.
\\
\textit{Urban Modeling and Simulation:} Developing urban simulation methods focusing on behavior and experience.
\\
\textit{City Science Living-Labs:} Developing a global network of urban research labs.
